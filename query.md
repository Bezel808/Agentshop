## 1. Smartwatch

### Hedonic Needs
- **H1S**: I want a smartwatch with a stylish and refined appearance that enhances my overall outfit and personal image.
- **H2S**: I want customizable watch faces, straps, and interface themes so that the watch reflects my personal taste.
- **H3A**: I want using the smartwatch to feel exciting and novel, like owning a trendy piece of technology.
- **H4A**: I want the smartwatch to help me express my lifestyle and taste in social settings.
- **H5A**: I want the design to make me feel happy to wear it and proud to show it to others.

### Utilitarian Needs
- **U1S**: I want the smartwatch to accurately track steps, heart rate, calories burned, and workout duration.
- **U2S**: I want reliable health monitoring functions such as sleep tracking, blood oxygen measurement, and heart rate alerts.
- **U3S**: I want notifications for calls, messages, and apps to appear clearly and in real time.
- **U4S**: I want long battery life so I do not need to charge it frequently.
- **U5S**: I want the system to run smoothly and connect stably with my phone and other devices.

### Balanced Needs
- **B1A**: I want the smartwatch to be both fashionable and highly functional.
- **B2S**: I want it to work well as both a stylish accessory and a practical device for work and exercise.
- **B3S**: I want it to look sleek and advanced while still being comfortable and lightweight on the wrist.
- **B4A**: I want it to be neither “good-looking but useless” nor “useful but unattractive.”
- **B5A**: I am willing to pay more for good design, but only if the core functions are also dependable.
